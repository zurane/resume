( About me )

What started as curiosity quickly evolved into a genuine passion for design

My journey into design began in my early high school days, sketching during free classes. What started as curiosity quickly evolved into a genuine passion for design, ultimately guiding my decision to pursue it as a career. After graduating, I chose to enroll at IIE Rosebank College to study graphic design. Two years into my studies, I secured an internship at Jetline Wits University, which eventually transitioned into full-time work where I gained valuable practical experience.

When the COVID-19 pandemic hit, it presented an unexpected opportunity to pick up essential skills that I could pair with my design experience. I started with coding, which eventually sparked my interest in web design and development.

With newfound time during lockdown, I immersed myself in learning web development through hands-on practice and portfolio building. I even caught on to areas like user experience and user interface design. After COVID, I began freelancing to make money, building websites and offering services in video editing, photography, and visual identity design for small businesses to sole proprietor entrepreneurs.

Today, I work as a web designer/administrator at GTIS SA, where I focus on user interface and user experience, and work with various industries and most recently, I completed a six-month full-stack web development certification through HyperionDev, further strengthening my technical foundation.

I consider myself a multidisciplinary designer, confident in my ability to adapt, learn, and take on diverse creative and technical projects.
